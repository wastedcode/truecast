# Modern & AI-era selling — the 2025–26 landscape

How the discipline actually runs now, the failure modes that kill deals, and where AI changes the work.

## The buyer changed — selling had to
- **~60–70% of the buying journey happens before a rep is engaged** (Gartner). By the time you're in the
  room, the buyer has researched; your value is *teaching* something they couldn't Google, not reciting
  the website (`teach-a-commercial-insight`).
- **The committee, not the contact, decides** — 5–11 stakeholders; single-threading is the most common
  silent deal-killer (`build-and-test-the-champion`).
- **The #1 competitor is "do nothing."** 40–60% of qualified pipeline ends in no-decision — and most of
  that is *fear of a wrong choice*, not happiness with the status quo. The modern winning move is to
  **de-risk** the decision, not to crank urgency (`beat-no-decision`).

## The failure modes that lose deals (what "great" avoids)
- **An empty pipe / failing to prospect** → no deals to work at all; the #1 reason sellers fail (Blount).
  Waiting for inbound, or only prospecting when the pipe is already dry, guarantees a future miss.
- **Product-pitching / feature-vomit** instead of diagnosing pain → loses to the status quo.
- **Skipping qualification** → effort poured into a deal with no economic buyer, no metric, no champion.
- **Single-threading** → the one friendly contact goes dark and the deal dies unseen.
- **Coach-as-champion** → betting on someone with goodwill but no power.
- **Reflexive discounting** → margin erosion, signals the price was fake, trains buyers to push.
- **Urgency on an indecisive buyer** → deepens the fear, pushes to no-decision.
- **Chasing zombies / counting compliments** → a forecast built on flattery.
- **Dodging the money / economic-buyer talk** → staying in a fog of polite interest.
- **Alligator arms** → doing the work, never asking for the commitment.
- **Hard sell** → closing a deal the customer regrets, selling something that won't deliver.
- **Happy ears / sandbagging in the CRM** → a forecast nobody can trust (~7% of teams hit 90%+ accuracy).
- **Spraying across channels** → proving none; dying on distribution, not product.
- **Mass AI spam** → torched deliverability, brand, and buyer trust.

## AI in sales (2025–26)
- **Market & adoption** — AI-SDR market ~$5–6B, growing >30%/yr; Gartner expects ~40% of enterprise apps
  to ship task-specific AI agents by end of 2026 (from <5% in 2025). ~45% of high-performing teams already
  run **hybrid** human+AI models: AI does research, prospect ID, first-touch personalization; humans do
  relationship and complex qualification.
- **Conversation intelligence** (Gong/Clari-style) — transcribes & analyzes calls; surfaces next-steps,
  competitor mentions, talk/listen ratio, MEDDPICC gaps. Teams using it close ~11 days faster with higher
  win rates on larger deals. Use it to coach the *next* call and keep the CRM honest.
- **Deal/pipeline intelligence** — flags at-risk, slipping, single-threaded, mis-staged deals. An **input**
  to an honest forecast, never the forecaster.
- **AI SDRs / agents** — research, prioritization, drafting. 100% of teams using them report time saved;
  but the risk is **scaling a bad motion into spam.**

### The non-negotiables when selling with AI (`sell-with-ai`)
1. **Human verifier on every buyer-facing claim** — a confident-wrong AI claim destroys trust instantly.
2. **Real personalization, not mail-merge cosplay** — generic/wrong "personal" lines are worse than plain
   ones; don't mass-blast.
3. **The honest-value bar holds** — no AI-manufactured urgency, fake references, or over-promises.
4. **Judgment stays human** — qualification, the forecast, the negotiation, the read on indecision.
5. **Find the motion first, then scale it with AI** — automating a bad motion just makes more bad motion.

## Where this persona sits with the others
- **product-manager** owns the problem/ICP/what-to-build; sales *sharpens the ICP from the buyer's side*
  and escalates an unsellable spec — but does not dictate the build.
- **product-marketer** owns the broadcast message/positioning/public copy/messaging; sales owns the 1:1
  read, the proactive 1:1 prospecting, and the channel *strategy* — and carries product-marketer's
  positioning into its 1:1 outreach. Coordinate, don't collide.
- **software-architect / software-engineer** own feasibility, timelines, and the build.
Consult the right lens; don't guess across them.
