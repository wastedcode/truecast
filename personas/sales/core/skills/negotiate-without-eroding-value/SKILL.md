# Negotiate without eroding value — trade, don't give

A reflexive discount is the most expensive habit in sales: it shrinks margin, **signals the original price
was fake**, trains the buyer to push every time, and rewards stalling. The principle (Voss, *Never Split
the Difference*): **trade value, don't give it away.** And the deeper truth: most "negotiation" problems
are really *qualification* problems — a buyer who saw the value (`probe-willingness-to-pay`) negotiates
terms, not whether to buy.

## Hold the line — trade, never give
- **Every concession buys something back.** If you move on price, you get something: a longer term, a case
  study/logo rights, a faster close, a bigger commitment, a reference, a payment-terms change. *"If we can
  hold the rate, can you commit by Friday / add a second team / give us a reference?"*
- **Defend with value, not justification.** When "your price is too high" lands, re-anchor on the
  quantified gap and cost-of-doing-nothing — don't apologize for the number.
- **Never discount to close a weak deal.** If you're tempted to cut price to rescue it, the problem is
  usually missing value or a missing economic buyer — go fix qualification, not the price.

## Voss tactics that work in B2B
- **Tactical empathy + labels** — "It seems like budget approval is the real hurdle" — name the emotion/
  concern to defuse it and surface the truth.
- **Calibrated questions** — "How am I supposed to do that?" / "What about this works for you?" — make
  *them* solve your constraint, shifting from positions to interests.
- **"No" is safe** — let them say no (it gives them control); avoid forcing a premature "yes." "Have you
  given up on this?" can restart a dark deal.
- **Mirror & silence** — repeat their last words, then wait; people fill silence with the real reason.

## The discipline
- Know your **walk-away** and your **trade list** *before* the call — improvising concessions under
  pressure is how value leaks.
- Discounting is a *pricing* lever, not a *closing* lever — if it's the only thing moving the deal, the
  value case failed.
- Margin you give away never comes back, and the next buyer hears about it. Protect the price; protect the
  business.
