# The audience dossier — the format

The shape of a buyer/audience dossier for messaging. The *content* — who this product is actually for —
you build from voice-of-customer (`find-the-customers-words`), the ICP (from **product-manager** + the
founder), and testing (`test-the-message`). An audience you invented and never tested against real people
is "generic marketing soup."

## One dossier per target segment — the fields
- **Name + one-line snapshot** — a memorable handle; who they are in a sentence.
- **The job they're hiring the product for (JTBD)** — functional + social + emotional, in *their* words.
- **Awareness stage** — unaware / problem-aware / solution-aware / product-aware / most-aware (drives what
  you say to them — `sequence-by-awareness`).
- **Role in the buy** — end user / champion / economic buyer / blocker (B2B); each needs its own message.
- **Today's alternative** — what they use now (competitor / spreadsheet / workaround / nothing) and what
  it costs them. This is what you position against (`differentiate-and-counter`).
- **Pains + gains** — the friction that makes them look; the outcome that makes them buy and stay.
- **Their literal language** — the exact words *they* use for the problem and the outcome (and the words
  they reject) — the raw material for headlines.
- **What makes them say yes** — the proof, the objection that must be answered, the trigger to act now.
- **Reach / value notes** — how many are like them, how acute the pain, whether a channel reaches them.

## How to use them
- **Lead with the beachhead** segment; re-weight the messaging house per segment (same core, different
  pillar emphasis and proof).
- **ICP vs. segment vs. persona:** the ICP is the *market* (product-manager owns it); a segment is a slice
  you point a message at; a persona is a concrete person within it.
- **Walk the persona through the message** before scaling — would *this* person, at *this* awareness stage,
  read the headline and get the aha?
- **Be honest about evidence.** Before real customers a persona is a hypothesis built from analogy +
  competitor language + founder intuition — label it, and make the first real prospects the test.
