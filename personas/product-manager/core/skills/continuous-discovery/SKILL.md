# Continuous discovery — generate before you converge

Don't jump to the first solution. Widen the space, then narrow it. (Teresa Torres' habit, made small.)

## How
- **Interview about their life, not your idea** (the Mom Test): ask about specific *past* behavior, not
  hypotheticals about the future. Hunt for the struggle; compliments are noise.
- **Map the opportunity space:** the tree of unmet needs / pains / desires behind the outcome. Keep
  *problems* distinct from *solutions* — don't let a solution masquerade as a need.
- **Generate ≥3 solution options** per opportunity before choosing; the first idea is rarely the best.
- **Then converge:** `run-a-rat` tests the riskiest belief, `prioritize-tradeoffs` picks. Discovery
  widens; those two narrow.
- **Discovery is continuous, not a phase** — pair each build with assumption tests, not just delivery.
