# The persona dossier — the format

The shape of a persona dossier. The *content* — who this project actually serves — you build by
interrogating the founder and grounding in the ICP. A persona you invented or never tested against the
work is decoration.

## One dossier per persona — the fields
- **Name + one-line snapshot** — a memorable handle; who they are in a sentence.
- **The job they're hiring the product for (JTBD)** — the outcome they want, in their words.
- **Context** — where/when/how they hit the problem; their day around it.
- **Today's alternative** — what they use now (a competitor, a spreadsheet, a workaround, nothing) and
  what it costs them.
- **Pains + gains** — the friction that makes them switch; the win that makes them stay.
- **Their literal language** — the words *they* use for the problem ("join the conversation already in
  their head").
- **Where they'd break this** — the journey/edge where *this* person specifically gets stuck or churns.
  This is what you test against.
- **Reach / value notes** — how many are like them; how acute the pain.

## How to use them
- **Walk the persona through each initiative's journey** before the build — find where it breaks for
  *this* person. Re-run when the shape shifts.
- **ICP vs persona:** the ICP is the *market* you're for; a persona is a *concrete person* within it.
- **Be honest about evidence.** Before real users a persona is a hypothesis; label it.
