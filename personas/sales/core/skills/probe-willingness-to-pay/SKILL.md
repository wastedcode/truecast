# Probe willingness-to-pay — the sharpest truth-probe

The price conversation reveals how urgent the pain *really* is faster than anything else — *WTP is one of
the strongest signals of product-market fit, stronger than engagement alone.* Reps who dodge the money
talk stay in a fog of polite interest; reps who probe it learn the truth. This is the persona's sharpest
probe, applied to both validation and a live deal.

## The probes
- **"What would you do if this disappeared tomorrow?"** — reveals whether it's a vitamin or a painkiller.
  A shrug = no real pain.
- **"What does this problem cost you today?"** — in money, time, risk, missed revenue. The quantified cost
  is the ceiling of what they'll pay and the spine of the business case (`run-the-discovery-call`).
- **"What would you budget to replace it?"** / **"How are you funding this — is there budget, or do we
  need to create it?"** — surfaces the real economic buyer and whether money exists.
- **"What are you spending on the workaround now?"** (people, tools, time) — anchors value against a real
  current cost, not zero.

## Cost of doing nothing
- The status quo is the default winner. Make **inaction** the expensive, risky option: quantify what
  *not* deciding costs per month, and what's at risk if nothing changes. This is the antidote to
  "we'll revisit next year."

## Value-based, not cost-plus
- Price to the **value created for the buyer**, not your cost +margin. If you solve a $1M problem, $100k is
  cheap; if you save 2 hours/month, it isn't.
- **Packaging is a truth-probe too** — what they're willing to pay *for which tier* tells you what they
  actually value. Anchor on value before you ever name a number.

## The discipline
- Don't quote a price before the gap is quantified — you'll either underprice real value or overprice thin
  value, and you'll have nothing to anchor to.
- A buyer who won't discuss budget at all hasn't felt the pain yet — go back to discovery, or it's a
  zombie.
- WTP is a *probe*, not a *negotiation* — learning what it's worth comes before holding the line on price
  (`negotiate-without-eroding-value`).
- In pre-revenue validation, a real WTP signal (a deposit, a signed LOI, a pre-pay) is **commitment** —
  worth more than ten "I'd definitely buy that"s (`validate-the-market`).
