# Know the buyer cold — earn the right to the conversation

A buyer can tell in 60 seconds whether you did the work. Generic outreach and "tell me about your
business" calls waste the one thing you can't get back — their attention. Pre-call research is how you
trade *teaching* for their time. ~60–70% of the buying journey now happens before they talk to a rep
(Gartner) — so when you do get the room, you have to add something they couldn't Google.

## What to know before the call
1. **Their world, not yours.** What does this person/role actually do all day, what are they measured on,
   what's hard about it right now? Read the ICP and personas *from the buyer's side* — who would pay, who
   would champion, who could block.
2. **The status quo.** What do they use today (a tool, a spreadsheet, a person, nothing)? What does that
   cost them — in money, time, risk, missed upside? The status quo is your real competitor, more often
   than another vendor.
3. **The trigger event.** Why might *now* be different — a new hire, a funding round, a regulation, a
   public miss, a reorg, a competitor move? No trigger usually means no urgency.
4. **The org & the money.** Who likely controls the budget (economic buyer), who'd feel the pain
   (champion), who'd resist (blocker)? Rough deal size and whether procurement/legal will be involved.
5. **A teaching angle.** One true, non-obvious thing about their problem you can lead with
   (`teach-a-commercial-insight`).

## The pre-call plan (5 minutes, every time)
- **Goal of the call** = a specific *advancement* (next meeting with X, access to data, a pilot scope) —
  not "build rapport."
- **3–5 hypotheses** about their pain you'll test (so the call is discovery, not interrogation).
- **The one insight** you'll teach.
- **The qualification gaps** you most need to close (which MEDDPICC letters are blank — see
  `qualify-the-deal`).

## The discipline
- Hypotheses, not a script — you're testing a thesis, ready to be wrong, not reading lines.
- If you can't find a plausible trigger or a status-quo cost, the honest move may be **don't pursue it yet**.
- Research scales with deal size: a 5-minute scan for a small deal, a deep account map for a strategic one.
  Over-researching a tiny deal is its own waste.
