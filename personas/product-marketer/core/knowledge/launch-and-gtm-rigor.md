# Launch, GTM & measurement rigor

The denser reference behind launches, segmentation, competitive intel, enablement, measuring impact, and
the AI-era PMM craft. Read on demand.

## The Foundational Four (and the modern stretch)
PMM's core responsibilities (Product Marketing Alliance, *State of PMM*): **Positioning & Messaging**
(~91%), **Product Launches** (~81%), **Sales Enablement** (~79%, the fastest-growing), **Market Research /
competitive intel** (~70%). PMMs increasingly also own **website/customer-experience touchpoints** and
report higher up the chain, with leadership judging them on **revenue/commercial impact**, not activity.

## PMM vs. adjacent roles (the lane)
- **Product management** defines *what* gets built + the roadmap + ICP/price. **PMM** defines *who it's for*
  and *how to position the value*. PMM pressure-tests scope from the market side; it doesn't set it.
- **Brand marketing** builds company reputation; **demand gen** attracts leads; **PMM** drives *adoption +
  retention* — the later funnel where positioning converts and where the message must be true.
- **Sales** owns the 1:1 deal and willingness-to-pay; **PMM** owns the broadcast message and arms the
  seller. Coordinate; don't collide.

## Segmentation & the beachhead
- Target **who cares most** about your differentiated value (work backwards from differentiation, not
  demographics). Lead with a **beachhead** segment, then expand (bowling-pin / GLEe: get-big → lead →
  expand). Even horizontal products launch into a beachhead.
- Separate the buy personas: **end user / champion / economic buyer / blocker** — each needs its own
  message and proof.

## Launch tiers (protect attention)
- **Tier 1 (marquee):** new product / category move / flagship — full motion; a few per year max.
- **Tier 2 (notable):** meaningful feature — blog/email/in-app/social + sales heads-up.
- **Tier 3 (continuous):** incremental — changelog/in-app/docs, no campaign.
Over-tiering (every release a Tier 1) trains the audience to ignore you. Most value ships Tier 3 in a
continuous-release world; reserve the marquee for moments that earn it.

## GTM plan components
Audience + segment + awareness stage → narrative + assets → motion (PLG / sales-led / community-led /
hybrid) + channels → enablement → **internal launch first** → measurement plan (defined before launch).

## Measuring PMM impact
- **Adoption, not applause:** feature adoption at **30 / 60 / 90 days** (tried → activated → retained),
  against a defined **activation/aha** moment — not impressions, press hits, or launch-day traffic.
- **Message-market fit / resonance:** the 5-second comprehension test; do target prospects say the value
  back in their own words; do they use *your* language or theirs.
- **Win rate vs. specific competitors** and **sales confidence** (internal survey) for positioning +
  enablement effectiveness.
- The launch retro: *"what we believed / what happened / what we'll change"* — lightweight, every launch.

## Competitive intelligence & win-loss
- **Win-loss analysis** is the truth source for why buyers actually decide — the only thing that beats
  internal opinion. Interview won *and* lost deals: why chose/didn't, who else was in the deal, what
  flipped it.
- **Battlecards** must be **living** (update per win/loss), honest (admit where the competitor wins), and
  field-sourced. Static, internally-written battlecards mislead the team.
- Position against the **real alternative** (often the status quo), not just the scary competitor.

## Sales enablement artifacts
Pitch deck (the narrative, not a feature tour), one-pager/leave-behind, demo script (proves the job),
objection handling (from win-loss), battlecards, talk track. **Train, don't just hand off**; **internal
launch before external.** Measure ramp time + sales confidence + win rate.

## The AI era (2026)
- PMM shifts from hand-producing every artifact to **strategic orchestration**: AI prototypes, tests,
  personalizes, and scales execution.
- **AI does:** variant generation (then test on real people), first drafts from the messaging house,
  voice-of-customer synthesis at scale, competitive/win-loss summarization, personalized delivery.
- **Humans keep:** positioning, category, segmentation, voice, the **truth** of every claim, and the
  judgment of what landed. AI used to *avoid thinking* accelerates mistakes and optimizes for clicks over
  meaning.
- **Slop is the failure mode:** generic, plausible-but-empty copy at scale erodes the brand faster than
  silence. Verify every AI claim against the shipped product.

## Common PMM failure modes (→ what the persona must catch)
Feature-dump / spec-list copy; internal jargon (one-sentence test fails); curse of knowledge; invented
adjectives / "generic marketing soup"; claims the product can't back (hype, especially "AI-powered
everything"); one pitch blasted at everyone (no segmentation, no awareness staging); accepting the default
category / comparing to nothing; over-tiering every launch; measuring impressions not adoption; copy
bolted on at the end instead of shaping scope; quietly writing *around* a positioning problem instead of
escalating; static battlecards from internal opinion; AI slop at scale; launching vaporware as if shipped.
