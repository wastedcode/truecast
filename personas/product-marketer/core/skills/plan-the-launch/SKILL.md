# Plan the launch — tier it, ship it, measure adoption

A launch is not an announcement — it's an orchestrated go-to-market that drives **adoption**. The
discipline is to **right-size the launch to the thing**: not everything can be Tier 1, and treating every
release as a marquee event burns the audience's attention and the team's credibility.

## Tier the launch (protect attention)
- **Tier 1 — marquee.** A new product, a category move, a flagship feature. Full motion: narrative,
  press/influencer, homepage, webinar, sales enablement, campaign. A few per year *at most*.
- **Tier 2 — notable.** A meaningful feature for a segment. Blog, email, in-app, social, sales heads-up.
- **Tier 3 — continuous.** Incremental releases. Changelog, in-app note, docs. No campaign.
Pick the tier *deliberately* and say no to over-tiering — the loud launch of a small thing trains the
audience to ignore you.

## The GTM plan (every tier scales this down)
1. **Audience + segment** — who is this for, what stage of awareness, what's the one message
   (`segment-the-audience`, `sequence-by-awareness`).
2. **The narrative + assets** — the story (`craft-the-narrative`), the announcement, landing page,
   in-product moment, the demo. Name the **real shipped thing**, never vaporware.
3. **Motion + channels** — PLG / sales-led / community-led / hybrid; pick the channels that actually reach
   the segment (coordinate the channel with **product-manager/sales** — you own the *message*, not the
   media buy).
4. **Enablement** — the team that sells/supports it is ready before launch (`enable-the-sale`).
5. **Internal launch first** — the company knows the message before the market does; misalignment leaks.
6. **The measurement plan** — defined *before* launch (below).

## Measure adoption, not applause
- The KPI is **feature adoption at 30 / 60 / 90 days** (% of target who tried, activated, retained) —
  *not* impressions, press hits, or launch-day traffic. Define activation (the aha moment) and track to it.
- Run the **"what we believed / what happened / what we'll change"** retro — a lightweight readout, every
  launch. Feed message learnings back (`test-the-message`).

## The discipline
- A launch with no adoption target is theater. Decide what success looks like before you spend.
- Coordinate timing/scope with **product-manager**; the launch can't promise what the build won't ship.
- Continuous-release motion is the norm now — most value ships Tier 3; reserve the marquee for the moments
  that earn it.
