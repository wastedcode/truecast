# Find the channel — distribution is co-equal with product

*"Poor sales, rather than a bad product, is the most common cause of failure"* and *"if you've invented
something new but haven't invented an effective way to sell it, you have a bad business"* (Thiel). Most
ventures die having gotten **zero** channels working — not for lack of product. You only need **one**
channel to work; the job is to find it cheaply before you bet the company on a guess.

## The Bullseye (Weinberg & Mares, *Traction*)
1. **Brainstorm** — for *every* one of the 19 channels, ask "what would this look like for us?" Don't skip
   the unobvious ones; the channel that works is often surprising. The 19: viral, PR, unconventional PR,
   SEM, social/display ads, offline ads, SEO, content marketing, email marketing,
   engineering-as-marketing, targeting blogs, business development, sales, affiliates, existing platforms,
   trade shows, offline events, speaking, community building.
2. **Rank** into three rings: which few are *most promising now* (inner), which are possible (middle),
   which are long shots (outer).
3. **Test the few — cheaply.** Run small, time-boxed experiments on the inner ring. The question is "can
   this channel reach our buyer at a cost that works?" — not "did we make a sale."
4. **Focus the ONE.** Once a channel shows traction, pour effort into it. Don't spray across all of them
   and prove none.

## Pick the GTM motion (early, pick one)
- **Sales-led** (higher ACV, complex, human-driven — the rest of these skills) · **product-led** (the
  product acquires/activates/expands itself; low-touch, self-serve) · **community-led** (a community is the
  top of funnel). Don't try to optimize two motions before you have scale.

## The discipline
- Spend real time on distribution — it's co-equal with product, not an afterthought (Traction's "50%
  rule": half your effort on traction, half on product, early).
- A channel test's deliverable is a **read** (CAC, reachability, conversion), not revenue — like a
  validation experiment.
- This is *channel strategy* (which channel, which motion) — the **broadcast message/positioning that runs
  on the channel is the product-marketer's** (identity lane); coordinate, don't collide.
