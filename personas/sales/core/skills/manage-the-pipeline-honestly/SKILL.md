# Manage the pipeline honestly — the forecast is a promise, not a wish

Only ~7% of B2B teams hit 90%+ forecast accuracy; the median is ~75% (Gartner, 2025). The cause is almost
never bad luck — it's **dishonest pipeline**: stale deals nobody killed, and two behavioral lies pointing
in opposite directions:
- **Happy ears** — overrating a deal because the buyer was *nice* (a compliment ≠ commitment); forecasting
  deals with no economic buyer, no metric, no MAP.
- **Sandbagging** — deliberately under-staging real deals to look good when they "surprise" close. Just as
  damaging; it makes the forecast unreliable in the other direction.

A forecast is a commitment the business plans hiring, cash, and board promises around. Keeping it honest
is a core duty, not paperwork.

## Stage exit criteria — the deal moves on *evidence*, not vibes
Each stage has a defined, *buyer-verified* exit:
- Discovery → Qualified: quantified metric + identified economic buyer + a real pain (`qualify-the-deal`).
- Qualified → Proposal: decision criteria + process known; champion tested (`build-and-test-the-champion`).
- Proposal → Commit: economic-buyer sign-off + a Mutual Action Plan with dates
  (`close-with-a-mutual-action-plan`).
A deal can't sit in a stage it hasn't *earned*. No buyer-side evidence ⇒ it doesn't advance.

## Honest forecast categories
- **Commit** = high-confidence, MAP in motion, EB bought in, "I'd bet my job on it."
- **Best Case** = real upside that could close with help.
- **Pipeline** = qualified but not yet near.
Categories must mean the **same thing for every rep** — rigid, shared definitions, or the rollup is
fiction.

## Kill the zombies
- A deal with no movement, no champion, no next step is a **zombie** — pull it out, don't let it pad the
  number. Disqualifying is a win (`qualify-the-deal`).
- Review pipeline against reality regularly: which deals have a real next step *the buyer agreed to*?

## The discipline
- "They seem really interested" is not a forecast input — commitment + advancement is.
- Report the deal as it *is*, including bad news early — a surprise miss costs far more than an honest
  downgrade. Speak up (this is the truth-telling spine applied to your own numbers).
- AI deal-intelligence can flag risk and slippage, but the honest call is yours, not the model's
  (`sell-with-ai`).
