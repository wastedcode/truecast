# Sequence by awareness — say what they're ready to hear

*"Before you write a single word of copy, answer one question: how much does your prospect already know?"*
(Eugene Schwartz, *Breakthrough Advertising*). Progressive disclosure of the message: reveal complexity in
the order the prospect can absorb it. The same true value is *said differently* at each stage.

## The five stages of awareness (Schwartz)
- **Unaware** — doesn't know they have the problem. Lead with a story or a striking observation about
  *their world*; name the latent pain. Don't mention the product. (Content, thought leadership, the "change
  in the world" from `craft-the-narrative`.)
- **Problem-aware** — feels the pain, doesn't know solutions exist. Lead with **empathy + problem-naming**;
  agitate the cost of the status quo. Earn trust before pitching.
- **Solution-aware** — knows solution types exist, not which to pick. Lead with **differentiation + proof**:
  why this *kind* of solution, and why yours within it (`differentiate-and-counter`).
- **Product-aware** — knows you, hasn't committed. Lead with **proof, specifics, objection-handling, the
  offer**: comparisons, case studies, guarantees, pricing clarity.
- **Most-aware** — ready, needs a reason to act now. Lead with the **offer + CTA + urgency**; get out of
  the way. (Here a feature/spec table is *useful* — they're comparison-shopping.)

## Market sophistication (the other dial)
How many times has this market heard claims like yours? (Schwartz's 5 stages.)
- **New market** → a plain, direct claim works ("does X").
- **Crowded market** → the plain claim is exhausted; you must **enlarge or out-specify the claim**, then
  **add a new mechanism** ("the *reason* it works"), then **identify with the prospect**. In a saturated
  category, a louder version of everyone's claim is invisible — change the mechanism or the frame, or
  reconsider the category itself (`write-the-positioning`).

## The discipline
- **Don't pitch the offer to the unaware** — the #1 mismatch (a "buy now" to someone who doesn't know they
  have the problem). Match copy to stage or it bounces.
- One product needs **multiple messages**, one per stage × segment — not one pitch blasted at everyone.
- Schwartz's rule: you don't *create* desire, you **channel existing desire** — find the desire already in
  their head and connect your product to it.
