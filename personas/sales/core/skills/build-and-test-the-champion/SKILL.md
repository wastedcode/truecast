# Build and test the champion — and never single-thread

A modern B2B deal has **5–11 stakeholders** (Gartner), yet ~78% of reps are **single-threaded** — betting
the whole deal on one friendly contact. When that contact goes dark, leaves, gets overruled, or simply
isn't powerful enough, the deal dies and you never saw it coming. The fix is to map the committee and
multi-thread, and to develop a champion who is *real*, not just nice.

## Map the buying committee
- **Champion** — has power *and* self-interest in your success; sells for you internally when you're not
  in the room.
- **Economic buyer** — controls the budget, gives final yes (often not the champion).
- **Coach** — gives you info and roots for you, but has no power. Useful, *not* a champion. The classic
  mistake is mistaking a coach for a champion.
- **Influencers / users / technical evaluators** — shape the decision criteria.
- **Blockers** — procurement, security, an incumbent's ally, a skeptic. Find them before they surface late.

## Develop — and TEST — the champion
A champion you haven't tested is a hope. Test for:
1. **Power** — can they actually influence the decision and the budget holder? (Title ≠ power.)
2. **Access** — will they get you to the economic buyer? "I'll handle the exec internally" with no path is
   a red flag.
3. **Self-interest** — what do *they* get when this wins (a metric, a promotion, a problem gone)? No
   personal win = no fight when it's hard.
4. **Will they sell?** — give them a small ask (share a doc internally, set up a meeting, get a question
   answered). If they don't deliver, they're a coach, not a champion. **Test before you bet the deal.**

## Multi-thread without burning your contact
- Map buying-group roles early; **ask for power by name** ("who else needs to be comfortable for this to
  go through?").
- Use **forwardable emails** — write a crisp, exec-ready note your champion can forward up, so you reach
  the economic buyer *with* the champion, not over their head.
- For deals >$50K target 5–7 engaged contacts across ≥3 roles; >$200K, 7–10. Keep a value touch on each
  thread.

## The discipline
- Single-threaded = at-risk, always. If you can name only one contact, that's the #1 next action.
- Never go over a champion's head silently — it burns the relationship that carries the deal.
- A deal with no path to the economic buyer is not advancing, no matter how warm the champion is
  (`qualify-the-deal`).
