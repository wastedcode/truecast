# Sales craft foundations

The deep craft behind how a great seller works — the references the skills lean on. The throughline:
**find the real buyer, prove the demand, and tell the commercial truth.**

> **Scope note (v2.0.0).** This persona's *active core* is the **founder-validation lens**: validate the
> market, know the buyer, run discovery, probe willingness-to-pay, find the channel. The qualification /
> committee / negotiation / forecast / prospecting / AI-selling craft below is the **optional
> enterprise-deal pack** — relevant only when you run a real quota-carried, multi-stakeholder pipeline. The
> references for the pack are kept here for that case; a solo founder validating an idea can ignore them.

## The one allegiance: the buyer's real problem + honest value, not the deal
Great selling is *helping someone make a good decision* — which sometimes means the honest "no."
- **Fitzpatrick (*The Mom Test*)** — *"Compliments are the fool's gold of customer learning."* Discount
  flattery; hunt **commitment** (time, reputation, money) + **advancement** (the next real step). No
  commitment = a **zombie lead**.
- **Blank (Customer Development)** — *"No business plan survives first contact with customers"*; *"there
  are no facts inside the building."* Early selling is the **validation experiment**, not closing.
- **Thiel (*Zero to One*)** — distribution is co-equal with product; *"poor sales, rather than a bad
  product, is the most common cause of failure."* Most ventures get **zero** channels working.

The active core stays deliberately in a **validation + distribution** lens (validate → know the
buyer → discover → probe willingness-to-pay → find the channel), keeping the truth-telling spine. The whole
working-deal arc — qualify → champion → teach → handle objections → de-risk indecision → close → negotiate
→ forecast → prospect — is available as the **optional enterprise-deal pack**, off by default for a solo
founder.

## Fill the pipe before you can work a deal (prospecting) — *enterprise pack*
- **Blount (*Fanatical Prospecting*)** — *"the number one reason for failure in sales is an empty pipeline,
  and the root cause of an empty pipeline is the failure to prospect."* The most-avoided craft, because the
  rejection is immediate; the pro makes it a **daily non-negotiable**. Target list off triggers → a
  multi-touch, multi-channel sequence → open on the buyer's problem → earn the first meeting. Quality and
  consistency, never blast volume — that's the line between a pro and spam (`prospect-and-open`).
- **Ross (*Predictable Revenue*) / Weinberg (*New Sales. Simplified.*)** — proactive, sequenced outbound
  off a tight target list and a problem-led opener; warm (referrals, champion intros, expansion) beats cold
  every time.

## Diagnose before you prescribe (discovery)
- **SPIN (Rackham)** — Situation → Problem → **Implication** (the engine: expand the cost of inaction) →
  Need-payoff (let them voice the value). Best reps spend 60–70% of the call asking.
- **Gap Selling (Keenan)** — current state → future state → **the quantified gap**; problem → impact →
  **root cause LAST**. No quantified gap = no deal. Skip the "walk me through your process" interrogation
  buyers hate.
- The Mom Test ported to a live deal: past behavior over hypotheticals; talk less, listen more.

## Qualify, or you'll drown in hope — *enterprise pack*
- **MEDDIC → MEDDPICC** — Metrics · Economic Buyer · Decision Criteria · Decision Process · **Paper
  Process** · Implicate the pain · Champion · **Competition** (incl. the status quo). The field-standard
  for complex B2B; most SaaS selling >$100K ARR uses a version. The blanks are your next-step list.
  Right-size: full machinery for enterprise, M/E/Champion for transactional. **Disqualify without guilt** —
  a fast "no" is a win.

## Teach, don't pitch (Challenger) — *enterprise pack*
- **Dixon & Adamson (*The Challenger Sale*, Gartner/CEB, 6,000 reps)** — ~54% of star reps in complex
  sales are Challengers: **teach** (a true commercial insight that reframes the buyer's problem),
  **tailor** (to the stakeholder's metric), **take control** (talk money, press for commitment, own the
  process). ~60–70% of the buying journey precedes rep contact — so add what they couldn't Google.

## The biggest modern loss: no-decision (JOLT) — *enterprise pack*
- **Dixon & McKenna (*The JOLT Effect*, 2.5M calls)** — 40–60% of qualified pipeline dies to **no
  decision**, and **56% of that is indecision/fear**, not status-quo bias. Indecision needs the **opposite**
  of urgency: **JOLT** = Judge the indecision · Offer a recommendation · Limit exploration · Take risk off
  the table. Pushing a scared buyer deepens the loss.

## The buying committee is a group, not a person — *enterprise pack*
- Modern deals: **5–11 stakeholders** (Gartner); ~78% of reps **single-thread** and lose when their one
  contact goes dark. **Multi-thread**, build and **test** a real champion (power + access + self-interest +
  will-to-sell), distinguish a **coach** (likes you, no power) from a champion, and reach the **economic
  buyer** via forwardable emails — never over the champion's head.

## Negotiate and price on value, not cost — *enterprise pack (the WTP probe itself is core)*
- **Voss (*Never Split the Difference*)** — tactical empathy, labels, calibrated questions; **trade, don't
  give** — every concession buys something back. A reflexive discount signals the price was fake and rewards
  stalling.
- **Willingness-to-pay** is one of the strongest PMF signals (stronger than engagement). Probe it
  directly; price to value, not cost-plus; quantify the **cost of doing nothing** so the status quo becomes
  the risky choice.

## Distribution & motion
- **Bullseye (Weinberg & Mares, *Traction*)** — brainstorm 19 channels → rank → cheap-test the few →
  focus the **one** that works. Pick the GTM **motion** (sales-led / product-led / community-led) — one
  early. Channel strategy is sales'; the **broadcast message on it is the product-marketer's**.

## Honest pipeline & the AI era — *enterprise pack*
- A forecast is a **promise** the business plans around; only ~7% of B2B teams hit 90%+ accuracy. Defeat
  **happy ears** (compliments-as-commitment) and **sandbagging** (under-staging) with stage exit criteria
  + shared forecast-category definitions; kill zombies.
- AI (conversation/deal intelligence, AI SDRs) multiplies *judgment* — used with a **human verifier**;
  it must never become mass spam, over-promise, or own the forecast call. The honest-value bar is
  unchanged.
