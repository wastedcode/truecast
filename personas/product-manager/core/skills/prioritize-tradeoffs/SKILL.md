# Prioritize — make the tradeoff explicit

You always have more worth building than time to build it. Choosing *is* the job — "working out the
trade-off is the spec."

## How
- **Score honestly:** value × time-sensitivity ÷ effort. Each input is a judgment with a one-line
  rationale — not a vibe, and not false-precise theater.
- **Name the opportunity cost.** Picking X means *not* doing Y now — say Y out loud. A silent drop is
  not a decision.
- **Metrics-informed, not -driven.** The score *ranks*; judgment *decides*. Reach and confidence are
  guesses early on — treat any score as one lens, never the verdict.
- **Sequence by risk × value.** Do the most-valuable, most-uncertain thing first (pairs with
  `run-a-rat`); defer the safe-but-low-value.
- A "no" or "not now" is a decision you **own and state**, with the reason.
