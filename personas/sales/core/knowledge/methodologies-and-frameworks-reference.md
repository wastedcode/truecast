# Methodologies & frameworks — the working reference

Quick-reference cheat sheets the skills lean on. Use the right tool for the deal; don't run enterprise
machinery on a self-serve sale.

## MEDDPICC (qualification — `qualify-the-deal`)
| Letter | Question it forces | Red flag if blank |
|---|---|---|
| **M** Metrics | What quantified economic impact does the buyer expect? | A feature shopper, no business case |
| **E** Economic Buyer | Who controls budget & gives final yes — have you a path to them? | Selling to a non-decider |
| **D** Decision Criteria | How will *they* decide (tech/business/relationship)? | You'll argue criteria at the end |
| **D** Decision Process | The actual steps & owners from interest → signed | Surprises, slippage |
| **P** Paper Process | Legal / security / procurement / MSA | +30–60 days "appears" at the end |
| **I** Implicate the pain | Is the cost of the problem & inaction urgent to them? | The status quo wins |
| **C** Champion | Power + self-interest, sells internally, **tested** | A coach, not a champion |
| **C** Competition | Other vendors **and the status quo / do-nothing** | Blindsided by "no decision" |

MEDDIC = the original 6 (no Paper, single Competition); MEDDPICC adds the 2nd P + C for enterprise. Switch
to the fuller version when ACV is high, the cycle is long, or legal/procurement is in play.

## SPIN (discovery question arc — `run-the-discovery-call`)
- **S**ituation (2–4 max — know most already) → **P**roblem (surface dissatisfaction) → **I**mplication
  (*the engine* — expand the cost of inaction) → **N**eed-payoff (let them voice the value).

## Gap Selling (discovery structure — `run-the-discovery-call`)
- **Current state → Future state → the Gap** (quantified). Sequence: **Problem → Impact → Root cause LAST.**
  Quantify the gap before pitching; skip the process-interrogation buyers hate.

## Challenger (the sale shape — `teach-a-commercial-insight`)
- **Teach** a true commercial insight that reframes their problem → **Tailor** to the stakeholder's metric
  → **Take control** (talk money, press for commitment, own the process). ~54% of star reps in complex
  sales are Challengers.

## JOLT (overcoming no-decision/indecision — `beat-no-decision`)
First **diagnose**: status-quo bias (→ create urgency) vs **indecision/fear** (→ de-risk; urgency
backfires). For indecision:
- **J**udge the indecision (valuation / information / outcome-uncertainty source) → **O**ffer a
  recommendation (tell them what you'd do) → **L**imit the exploration (fewer options; pre-empt questions)
  → **T**ake risk off the table (pilot, phased rollout, guarantee, references).
- Why it matters: 40–60% of qualified pipeline dies to no-decision; 56% of that is indecision, not status
  quo.

## Prospecting & cadence (`prospect-and-open`)
- **An empty pipe is the #1 reason sellers fail** (Blount, *Fanatical Prospecting*); the root cause is the
  *failure to prospect* — a daily non-negotiable, not a panic activity. Pipe filled today closes ~a
  quarter out.
- **Sequence, not a touch:** a multi-touch cadence over ~2–3 weeks across **phone + email + social/
  referral**; most positive replies come after several touches, most reps quit after 1–2.
- **Open on the buyer's problem + a trigger event**, one clear ask (a short discovery call); tight,
  human, *researched* beats long/generic — mail-merge "personalization" is worse than plain.
- **Warm > cold:** referrals, champion intros, existing-customer expansion are the highest-yield. Goal =
  *qualified meetings*, not activity. The line vs. spam is quality + consistency, not volume
  (`sell-with-ai`, `find-the-channel`).

## Multi-threading / buying committee (`build-and-test-the-champion`)
- Roles: **Champion** (power + self-interest, sells for you) · **Economic Buyer** (budget, final yes) ·
  **Coach** (info + goodwill, no power) · **Influencers/Users/Technical** · **Blockers**.
- Test a champion: power · access to EB · self-interest · will-they-sell (give a small ask). 5–11
  stakeholders typical; >$50K → 5–7 contacts / ≥3 roles, >$200K → 7–10. Use **forwardable emails** to
  reach the EB *with* the champion.

## Objection taxonomy (`handle-the-objection`)
**B**udget · **A**uthority · **N**eed · **T**iming. Method: listen → **label & clarify** → confirm/isolate
→ answer the *real* one. "Too expensive" may be value, funding, trust, or fear in disguise. ~67% of lost
deals had an unaddressed objection.

## Voss negotiation (`negotiate-without-eroding-value`)
- **Trade, don't give** (every concession buys something back) · tactical empathy + **labels** ("it seems
  like…") · **calibrated questions** ("how am I supposed to do that?") · "**No**" is safe · mirror +
  silence. Never discount to rescue weak qualification.

## Willingness-to-pay probes (`probe-willingness-to-pay`)
- "What would you do if this disappeared tomorrow?" · "What does this cost you today?" · "What would you
  budget to replace it?" · "What's the workaround costing you now?" Price to **value**, not cost-plus.

## Bullseye — distribution (`find-the-channel`)
- Brainstorm → rank (3 rings) → cheap-test the few → focus the **one**. The 19 channels: viral, PR,
  unconventional PR, SEM, social/display ads, offline ads, SEO, content marketing, email,
  engineering-as-marketing, targeting blogs, BD, sales, affiliates, existing platforms, trade shows,
  offline events, speaking, community.

## Forecast categories (`manage-the-pipeline-honestly`)
- **Commit** (bet-my-job, EB bought, MAP moving) · **Best Case** (real upside, needs help) · **Pipeline**
  (qualified, not near). Shared rigid definitions across all reps, or the rollup is fiction. Median B2B
  forecast accuracy ~75%; only ~7% hit 90%+.
