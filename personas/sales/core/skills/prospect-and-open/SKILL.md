# Prospect and open — fill the pipe; an empty pipe is the #1 reason sellers fail

Every skill that follows — discovery, qualification, champion, close — operates on a deal that *already
exists*. **Prospecting is how the deal comes to exist at all**, and it is the single most-avoided activity
in the craft. *"The number one reason for failure in sales is an empty pipeline, and the root cause of an
empty pipeline is the failure to prospect"* (Jeb Blount, *Fanatical Prospecting*). Reps procrastinate on
it because it's uncomfortable and the rejection is immediate — so they bury themselves in admin, "research,"
and warm deals until the pipe runs dry 90 days later. The professional treats prospecting as a
**non-negotiable daily discipline**, not a thing to do when desperate.

This is the offensive counterpart to `find-the-channel` (which channel reaches buyers *at all*) and
`sell-with-ai` (the guardrail against spam). Here you actually *do* the outreach — at quality, not volume.

## Build the target list (don't "boil the ocean")
- Start from the ICP and the buyer-side read (`know-the-buyer-cold`): a tight list of accounts that match
  the pattern that already buys, not a giant scraped list.
- For each account, identify the likely champion *and* the likely economic buyer by name and role — you'll
  multi-thread from day one (`build-and-test-the-champion`).
- Prioritize on **trigger events** — funding, a new exec hire, a reorg, a public miss, a regulation, a
  competitor move. A timely, relevant reason-to-reach-out beats ten cold-out-of-nowhere touches.

## Sequence the outreach — multi-touch, multi-channel
- **It takes a sequence, not a touch.** A single email is a coin flip you'll lose. Run a structured
  cadence over ~2–3 weeks across **phone, email, and social/referral** — most positive replies come after
  several touches, and most reps quit after one or two.
- **Lead with the buyer's problem, not your product.** The opening line earns the next five seconds:
  a relevant trigger + a specific problem you see in their world + a hint of an insight
  (`teach-a-commercial-insight`) — never "I wanted to introduce our platform."
- **Tight and specific beats long and generic.** A crisp, human, *researched* message; one clear ask (a
  short call), one idea. If the "personalization" is a mail-merge token, it's worse than plain.
- **Vary the channel and the angle** across the sequence; a "break-up" / permission-to-close touch at the
  end ("should I close your file on this?") often pulls the most honest reply.

## Open the cold conversation (call or first reply)
- **Earn the time, then get out of the way.** Name why you're calling them specifically (the trigger),
  state a problem you suspect they have, and ask permission to ask two questions — then run discovery
  (`run-the-discovery-call`). Don't pitch; you're trading a relevant insight for a meeting.
- **The only goal of the open is the next step** — a real, calendared discovery meeting (an *advancement*),
  not a "yes I'm interested." Book it on the call.
- **Handle the reflex brush-off** ("we're all set," "send me info") as an objection to understand, not a
  wall (`handle-the-objection`): "Most people I talk to said that right up until [trigger] — can I ask
  one question?"

## The discipline
- **Protect prospecting time on the calendar** and hit it daily — the pipe you fill today closes a quarter
  from now. The cause of next quarter's miss is this quarter's skipped prospecting.
- **Quality and consistency, not blast volume.** This is the line that separates a pro from spam: relevant,
  researched, human, sequenced — `sell-with-ai` can *assist* the research and drafting, but a person owns
  every send, and you never torch the domain or the brand for short-term volume.
- **Warm beats cold every time** — referrals, intros from your champion, and existing-customer expansion
  are the highest-yield prospecting; ask for them deliberately, don't wait for them.
- **Honest from the first word.** No fake "re:" subject lines, no invented mutual connections, no
  manufactured urgency. The buyer's real problem, told straight — even in the opener (identity).
- The deliverable is **qualified meetings**, not activity. Measure replies→meetings→qualified, and feed the
  read back into which channel and which message work (`find-the-channel`, `manage-the-pipeline-honestly`).
