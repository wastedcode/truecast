# User interviews — talk to users, the right way

The cardinal PM skill. "Talk to more users (actually do it)" (Lenny Rachitsky) is the most common fix
for a stuck product. But naive interviews produce *lies you want to hear* — so run them by the **Mom
Test** (Rob Fitzpatrick) and keep them **continuous** (Teresa Torres), not a one-off phase.

## The Mom Test — get truth, not flattery
Even your mom will lie to protect your feelings. So:
1. **Talk about their life, not your idea.** Never pitch; if they don't know you're testing an idea, they
   can't flatter it. Your idea coming up is a failure of discipline.
2. **Ask about specific past behavior, not future hypotheticals.** "When did you last hit this? Walk me
   through it" — not "would you use…?" Hypotheticals and opinions are worthless; history is data.
3. **Ask for stories, not opinions.** "Tell me about the last time…" surfaces what actually happened,
   the workarounds, the cost, the emotion.
4. **Find where they already spend time and money.** Real demand leaves a budget and a workaround trail;
   "that's cool" leaves nothing.
5. **Talk less, listen more.** Shut up after the question. Dig into the *why* behind each story; chase
   the struggle, not the compliment.
6. **Anchored, not leading.** Avoid questions that telegraph the answer.

## Make it continuous (Torres)
- **Weekly touchpoints** with users by the people building — small and ongoing, so decisions ride fresh
  evidence, not a stale quarterly report. (For a tool with no external users yet, this collapses to your
  own observed usage + reaching the few real prospects — say so; don't pretend n=1 is a market.)
- **Capture interview snapshots:** one page per interview (who, the story, key quotes, the surprise) so
  insight compounds and feeds the opportunity tree (`continuous-discovery`).
- **Recruit continuously** so you're never blocked waiting to schedule.

## From interview → insight
- Separate **observation** (what they did/said) from your **interpretation** — don't smuggle a solution
  in as a need.
- Hunt for the **opportunity** (the unmet need/pain), place it on the OST, and let it earn ≥3 solution
  options before you converge with `run-a-rat`.
- One vivid story beats a survey of opinions; but pair qual with the quant (`define-success-metrics`) —
  behavior data says *what*, interviews say *why*.

## The discipline
- A persona/spec you haven't tested against a real person is decoration (`pressure-test-personas`).
- Beware your own enthusiasm and the loudest customer; the goal is the *no* as much as the yes.
- "We already know what users want" is the cardinal risk — the cheapest 5 conversations beat the
  confident assumption every time.
