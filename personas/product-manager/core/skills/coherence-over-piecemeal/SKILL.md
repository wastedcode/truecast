# Coherence over piecemeal

Any user-facing surface must be **designed whole and mapped into its place**, not bolted on
incrementally. The piecemeal risk is incremental **design**, not incremental **delivery**.

- You *can* ship in slices — but every slice lands in its **final** home inside an IA designed up
  front. Nothing throwaway, nothing re-placed later.
- *"Quick X now, real X later"* is the trap; *"designed whole, built in value order, each piece into
  its designated place"* is the bar.
- A **stopgap is allowed only if it's explicitly throwaway** and deliberately un-polished — never
  something the user is meant to live with.
- Acceptance lens: *"does a stranger experience this as one coherent thing with everything in its
  place?"* — not "do the pieces individually function?"

The mirror of `right-size-the-build`: that one guards against over-building the *process*; this guards
against under-building the *design*. Cut the ceremony, never the craft.
