# Test the message — message-market fit is earned, not asserted

The team is the worst judge of its own copy (the curse of knowledge). A message is a **hypothesis** until
real target customers react to it. **Message-market fit** is the goal: the right people read it and
*immediately get it and want it*. You measure that; you don't assert it from internal consensus.

## The method
1. **The 5-second test (comprehension).** Show the page/headline to a target customer for 5 seconds, hide
   it, then ask: *What does this do? Who's it for? What would you do next?* If they can't say back the
   value in their own words, it failed — rewrite, retest. (No jargon survives this test.)
2. **Resonance, on real prospects.** Get the message in front of the **actual segment** (not friends, not
   the team). Watch for: do they use *your* words back, or theirs? Does it trigger a story ("oh, that's
   like when I…")? Do they lean in or glaze over? One vivid "that's exactly my problem" beats ten polite
   "looks nice."
3. **Test the alternatives, not just yours.** Show 2–3 headline/angle options; the winner is the one
   prospects *choose and explain*, not the one the founder likes. Cheap channels: a landing-page A/B, a
   five-second tool, a few real customer calls, an ad with two variants.
4. **Read behavior over opinion** (Mom-Test logic). "I'd use that" is a compliment, not data; a click, a
   signup, a *"can I have it now?"*, a referral is signal. Discount what people *say* in favor of what they
   *do*.
5. **Close the loop into the message.** Feed wins back into the messaging house and the customer-words
   bank (`find-the-customers-words`); kill the angles that didn't land.

## The discipline
- **Pre-users, label it a hypothesis** (`write-the-positioning`) — from analogy + competitor language +
  founder intuition — and make the first real users the test. A sample of one (the founder) is **not**
  message-market fit; say so, never pretend dogfood is a market.
- A launch that underperforms is usually a **message** problem before a product problem — retest the
  message before blaming the product or the channel.
- Test *before* you scale spend. Scaling an unvalidated message is buying reach for a page that doesn't
  convert.
