# Message the job, not the feature — sell the hole, not the drill

*"People don't want a quarter-inch drill; they want a quarter-inch hole"* (Levitt, via Christensen's
JTBD). Customers **hire** a product to make progress in their life. Your job is to translate every
capability into the **outcome** they're hiring for — the functional job, and the **social** ("how I look")
and **emotional** ("how I feel") jobs riding alongside it.

## The method
1. **Find the job.** For each feature, ask: *what is the customer trying to get done, and what does
   success feel like?* Use the customer's words (`find-the-customers-words`), not the spec's. Loom didn't
   sell "async video with AI" — it sold *"save 20 minutes a day."*
2. **Translate feature → benefit → outcome.** Feature = what it is. Benefit = what it does for them.
   Outcome = the progress in their life/work. Lead with outcome; benefit supports; feature is proof. The
   feature is the *reason to believe*, not the headline.
3. **Apply the one-sentence test.** A customer can explain your value in **one sentence with zero internal
   jargon.** If they can't, you're still feature-first — rewrite. ("It's a vector DB with HNSW indexing"
   → "find the right answer in your docs in milliseconds.")
4. **Cover all three job dimensions** where they matter — the emotional/social job is often the real buy
   trigger ("I won't look incompetent in the meeting"), and most copy ignores it.
5. **Cut the curse of knowledge.** Read it as a stranger who has never seen the product. Every acronym,
   feature name, and internal term is a tax on understanding — spend it only when it earns trust with this
   audience (`sequence-by-awareness` — sophisticated audiences value precise terms; novices don't).

## The discipline
- **Outcomes, not menus.** Customers buy outcomes; a feature grid is for the comparison shopper at the
  most-aware stage, not the headline.
- **One job per artifact.** Trying to sell every job at once sells none. Pick the dominant job for this
  surface and this segment.
- A capability with no nameable job the customer cares about is a flag *for product*, not a copy problem —
  raise it (`write-the-positioning` step: drop attributes with no value).
