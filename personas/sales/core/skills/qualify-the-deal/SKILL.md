# Qualify the deal — separate real deals from hope

Time is the only thing a seller can't make more of, and the most expensive mistake after selling the
wrong thing is **pouring effort into a deal that was never going to close.** Qualification is how you find
that out early. MEDDPICC is the field-standard for complex B2B (used by most SaaS selling >$100K ARR) — it
turns "I have a good feeling" into a checklist of what's known and what's still blank.

## MEDDPICC — what each letter forces you to know
- **M — Metrics.** The quantified economic impact the buyer expects (the gain or the cost avoided). No
  metric = no business case = a feature shopper, not a buyer. (Comes from `run-the-discovery-call`.)
- **E — Economic Buyer.** The one person who controls the budget and can say yes. If you haven't met them
  or don't have a path to them, the deal is not qualified — it's a wish.
- **D — Decision Criteria.** How *they* will decide (technical, business, relationship). Win by shaping
  the criteria toward your strengths, early — not arguing them at the end.
- **D — Decision Process.** The actual steps from "interested" to "signed" — who's involved, in what
  order, by when.
- **P — Paper Process.** Legal, security review, procurement, MSA, vendor onboarding. The silent
  deal-killer that "appears" in the last week and adds 30–60 days. Surface it *now*, not later.
- **I — Implicate the pain.** Have you made the cost of the problem (and of inaction) real and urgent to
  the buyer? Tie back to Metrics.
- **C — Champion.** A person with power and self-interest who sells for you when you're not in the room —
  built and **tested** (`build-and-test-the-champion`). A coach who likes you is not a champion.
- **C — Competition.** Including the status quo and "do nothing," not just other vendors. What's the
  alternative they're weighing, and why you?

(MEDDIC = the original; MEDDPICC adds the second **P**aper process + **C**ompetition for enterprise. Use
the fuller version when ACV is high, the cycle is long, or legal/procurement is involved.)

## How to apply it
1. **Score the deal** — which letters are solid, which are blank or assumed? The blanks are your next-step
   list, not a paperwork exercise.
2. **Right-size it.** A small, transactional deal needs M/E/Champion and little else; a $250K enterprise
   deal needs all eight, tracked. Don't run enterprise machinery on a self-serve sale.
3. **Disqualify without guilt.** No economic buyer + no metric + no champion after real effort = walk away
   or de-prioritize. A fast "no" frees you for a real deal. Disqualifying is a *win*, not a failure.

## The discipline
- A proposal or a forecast-commit before qualification is a **zombie** — re-qualify before you invest more.
- "They seem really interested" is not a MEDDPICC letter. Compliments aren't qualification (`handle-the-objection`).
- Update the score as the deal moves; a deal can *de*-qualify (champion leaves, EB changes) — catch it.
