# Close with a mutual action plan — earn the yes, don't trick it

Closing is not a magic phrase at the end; it's the natural result of good discovery, qualification, and a
real champion. The two failure modes are opposite: the **hard sell** (manipulative pressure that wins a
deal the customer regrets) and **"alligator arms"** (doing all the work but being too afraid to actually
ask for the commitment). The professional move is a **Mutual Action Plan** plus a clear, direct ask.

## The Mutual Action Plan (MAP)
A jointly-owned, written plan of the steps from "interested" to "live," built **backward from the
buyer's desired go-live date.**
1. **Anchor on their date.** "You want this live by Q3 — let's work back from there."
2. **List every step**, with owner and date, on *both* sides: technical validation, security review,
   business case to the EB, **legal/MSA, procurement, vendor onboarding** (the Paper Process —
   `qualify-the-deal` — that silently adds 30–60 days), signature, implementation.
3. **Make it mutual.** Both sides have tasks; the buyer's commitments to the plan *are themselves
   advancement signals* — agreeing to the MAP is a real yes.
4. **Drive it.** Reference it every conversation; a slipping step is an early warning, not a surprise.

## Commitment + advancement, never compliments
- The deliverable of every interaction is **advancement** (a concrete next step) and **commitment** (they
  give up time, reputation, or money). "This looks great, we love it" with no next step is a *compliment* —
  fool's gold (Fitzpatrick).
- Define the next *real* step every time; "we'll circle back" is not advancement.

## Ask for the commitment
- **Actually ask.** "Based on everything, are you ready to move forward?" / "What needs to be true for us
  to sign by [date]?" Earning the right to ask and then not asking wastes it.
- A trial close mid-process ("if this checks out, is there anything stopping us?") surfaces hidden
  objections (`handle-the-objection`) before the end.
- If the answer is a stall, it's likely indecision — `beat-no-decision`, don't pressure.

## The discipline
- A close with no MAP and no economic-buyer sign-off is a happy-ears forecast, not a deal
  (`manage-the-pipeline-honestly`).
- Never close a deal you know won't serve the customer to hit a number — the honest "no" / "not yet"
  protects the relationship and the brand (identity).
- The best close is anticlimactic: by the time you ask, the buyer already decided, because you de-risked
  and built the case together.
